DevOps Article

DevOps is a model of company culture and development practice. It is characterized by collaboration across all levels of a company, rapid deployment of software, and continuous improvement. DevOps was created to bridge the gap between software developers and IT operations. DevOps encourages developers and IT to collaborate so that systems remain stable with rapid deployment of new technology. To accomplish the goals of DevOps, three main techniques are used: automation, continuous testing, and continuous deployment. Automation is accomplished by using automated tools and utilities that are either developed ‘in house’ or sourced from elsewhere. continuous testing is a quality by design model, where QA engages with a project from the inception, rather than after all the code is written. Continuous deployment is the rapid deployment of new features or improvements. Continuous deployment requires that new features are delivered tested and secure.

Code Reads #12: Big Ball of Mud

I found that the article summarized a very important lesson, put something out that works and worry about form later (or only as necessary). Often in business, first to the market is more advantageous than being the best. Once you put out a working system, then with feedback of users, you can iteratively add improvements as necessary. The main disadvantage to this approach seems to be that maintenance is difficult. I would also think keeping software designed as a ball of mud running would be difficult if the people who created it left the organization with little warning.
